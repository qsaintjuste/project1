About Me

Academic Background

I am currently pursuing a bachelor’s degree in Applied Cybersecurity and Information Technology Management at Illinois Institute of Technology. I previously completed my Associate degree in Cybersecurity with a GPA of 3.66 and earned the Illinois Tech Purpose Scholarship for academic excellence and leadership potential.

My coursework includes Ethical Hacking, Cybercrime & Incident Response, Cloud Computing, Scripting for Cybersecurity, Information Security Systems Analysis, and Networking fundamentals. I am also preparing for the CompTIA Security+ certification to strengthen my security foundation.

Professional Experience & Leadership

Beyond academics, I have built strong leadership and administrative skills through professional experience. At Chicago Public Schools, I supported special education students, tracked behavioral data in Excel, and generated performance reports that influenced academic planning.

I have also worked in management roles where I supervised teams, handled scheduling logistics, and resolved high-pressure situations. These roles strengthened my communication, adaptability, and decision-making skills.

Personal Growth & Discipline

Discipline defines my life. Fitness is not just physical — it represents mental strength and consistency. Inspired by figures like David Goggins, I believe in pushing past limits and choosing discomfort in order to grow.

Daily structure, early workouts, goal tracking, and continuous learning are habits I practice intentionally. I do not chase motivation — I build systems that make success inevitable.

Long-Term Vision

Long-term, I aim to become a respected cybersecurity professional who combines technical expertise with leadership and resilience. I want to contribute to protecting digital infrastructure and help organizations operate securely in an evolving technological landscape.

I believe success is built daily — not occasionally. Every skill, experience, and challenge compounds into long-term growth.
